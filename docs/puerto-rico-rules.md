# Puerto Rico — Rules

*Designer: Andreas Seyfarth · © 2001/10 Andreas Seyfarth · © 2002/11/13/19 Ravensburger / Rio Grande Games*

> **About this transcription.** This document is transcribed from two uploaded editions of the rulebook:
> the **Deluxe edition** (16 pages — base game + 2-player rules + Expansion I "The New Buildings" + Expansion II "The Nobles") and the
> **original 2002 edition** (12 pages — base game only).
> The base-game text below follows the Deluxe edition, which is a superset. Differences between the two editions are catalogued in the
> [Edition Differences](#edition-differences-cross-check) section at the end. The most important wording difference: the Deluxe edition calls the
> special buildings **beige** and stores spare colonists on the **windrose**, whereas the original calls the same buildings **violet** and stores
> spare colonists in **San Juan**. These are cosmetic; the mechanics are identical.

---

## Goal

The game is played over several rounds. In each round, each player chooses one of seven different roles and, thereby, offers all players, in clockwise order, the action associated with that role.

For example, with the settler, players can place new plantations, on which — with the help of the craftsman — players can produce goods. Players can then sell these goods to the trading house with the trader, or, with the captain, ship them to the old world. With the money earned from such sales, the players, with the builder, may build buildings in the city, and so on.

The player who best manages the changing roles with their associated actions and special privileges will achieve the greatest prosperity and the highest respect, and thereby win the game. **The winner is the player who earns the most victory points.**

## Preparation (Basic Game: 3–5 Players)

Place the game board with the large bank in the middle of the table. Place all the basic buildings on their respective spaces (long descriptions for new players, or the shorter explanations for experienced players, face up). Sort the 54 doubloons by value and place them on the bank.

**Each player takes:**

- **1 player board** (their play area).
- **Money** (stored on the windrose, visible to all): 3 players → 2 doubloons; 4 players → 3 doubloons; 5 players → 4 doubloons.
- **1 plantation tile**, placed face-up on any of their 12 island spaces.

Choose a starting player by any method. That player takes the **Governor placard** and a small **indigo** tile. The others take, in clockwise order:

- **3 players:** 2nd → indigo / 3rd → corn
- **4 players:** 2nd → indigo / 3rd & 4th → corn
- **5 players:** 2nd & 3rd → indigo / 4th & 5th → corn

**Supply setup (scaled by player count):**

| | 3 players | 4 players | 5 players |
|---|---|---|---|
| VP chips | 75 | 100 | 126 |
| Cargo ships | 4, 5, 6 spaces | 5, 6, 7 spaces | 6, 7, 8 spaces |
| Colonists (supply) | 55 | 75 | 95 |
| Role placards | all except both prospectors (6) | all except one prospector (7) | all 8 |
| Colonist ship | 3 colonists | 4 colonists | 5 colonists |

Also lay out: all 8 quarry tiles (face-up); the rest of the plantation tiles (face-down, shuffled into 5 stacks); a face-up row of plantation tiles equal to **players + 1**; all goods tokens (5 sorted piles); the trading house. Return unused material to the box.

## Playing the Game

The game lasts several rounds (about 15). Each round:

1. The **Governor** takes one role placard, places it face-up beside their board, and takes its action. Then each other player, clockwise, takes that same action once.
2. The Governor's left neighbor then chooses one of the **remaining** role placards and the action goes around again — and so on, until every player has taken exactly one role placard and all the chosen actions have resolved.
3. Place **one doubloon on each of the three unused role placards**. Return the used placards to the table.
4. The Governor placard passes to the left neighbor, who becomes the new Governor. Continue as before.

### The Roles

Each role grants its chooser a **privilege**, plus an **action** that every player may take in clockwise order starting with the chooser (*exception: prospector*).

Basic rules for all role placards:

- If a placard has doubloons on it, its chooser also takes those doubloons (in addition to privilege and action).
- The action is always taken first by the chooser, then clockwise.
- A player must always take a placard on their turn, but may decline to use its action or privilege. The others still get their turns at the action.
- The action is optional (*exception: captain*).
- A placard stays in front of its chooser until the end of the round and cannot be taken by anyone else that round.

### The Settler — *place new plantations*

**Action:** each player takes and places a plantation tile.
**Privilege:** the settler may take and place a **quarry** instead.

The settler takes either a quarry (privilege) or a face-up plantation tile, placing it on an empty island space. Then each other player, clockwise, may take **one face-up plantation tile** (not a quarry — *exception: construction hut*) and place it on an empty island space.

Finally the settler discards untaken face-up plantation tiles (face-up on the discard stack) and draws new ones from the face-down stacks to refill the row to **players + 1**.

Notes:
- Remember the special functions of the hacienda, construction hut, and hospice.
- If the face-down stacks run out, place what remains, then reshuffle the discards into five new face-down stacks and refill the face-up row. If still short, later rounds may go without.
- Tile placement on the island is purely positional and tiles can never be removed.
- A player with all 12 island spaces filled takes no further tiles in settler phases.
- Taking a plantation is voluntary.
- With no quarry tiles left, the settler can't use the privilege and the construction hut's function is unavailable.

### The Mayor — *arrival of new colonists*

**Action:** each player takes and places colonists in turn order.
**Privilege:** the mayor may take one additional colonist (from the **supply**, not the ship).

Tiles (plantation, quarry, or building) have 1–3 circles. A tile with at least one colonist is **occupied**; only occupied tiles function (*exception: VP value*).

The mayor first takes one colonist from the supply (privilege). Then, starting with the mayor and going clockwise, players take colonists one at a time from the **colonist ship** until it is empty.

Players may place their new colonists, together with all previously acquired ones, on any empty circles — i.e. colonists may be rearranged each mayor phase. Colonists that don't fit are **stored on the windrose** until a later mayor phase. (Empty circles must be filled if possible; you may not stash colonists on the windrose while you have open circles. Colonists are only placed during the mayor phase.)

As a last duty, the mayor refills the colonist ship: one colonist from the supply for **each empty building circle across all players** (empty plantation/quarry circles don't count), but always **at least as many as there are players**.

Notes:
- Usually everyone places simultaneously; if order matters, go mayor-first then clockwise.
- A forgotten ship refill defaults to the minimum (= number of players) next time.
- If the colonist supply is empty, no privilege and no ship refill.

### The Builder — *build buildings*

**Action:** each player may build one building.
**Privilege:** the builder pays 1 doubloon less.

The builder may immediately build **one** building, paying 1 doubloon less than printed cost, placing it on an empty city space (a **large building** needs two adjacent empty spaces). Then each other player, clockwise, may build one building at normal cost.

- No player builds more than one building per round.
- If the builder builds nothing, they get no privilege doubloon.
- You can't build past 12 city spaces.

#### Quarry (cost reduction)

Each **occupied** quarry reduces a building's cost by 1 doubloon, capped by the building's column on the board: column 1 → max 1, column 2 → max 2, column 3 → max 3, column 4 → max 4. The builder's −1 privilege stacks on top of quarry reductions, but cost can't go below 0. (Example, 3 occupied quarries: construction hut 1; office 3; harbor 5; city hall 7.) Remember the university's function.

### The Craftsman — *produce goods*

**Action:** all players take goods from the supply per their production.
**Privilege:** the craftsman takes one extra good (of a kind they produced).

The craftsman produces first (placing goods on the windrose), then the others clockwise. As a last duty the craftsman takes one additional good of a kind they produced, as the privilege.

Notes:
- Remember the factory.
- If a produced good's supply is exhausted, that good is simply not taken (no substitute).
- No production → no extra good.
- *The craftsman is the riskiest role: watch that you don't help opponents more than yourself.*

### The Trader — *sell goods*

**Action:** each player may sell at most one good.
**Privilege:** the trader gets +1 doubloon on their sale.

The trader may sell one good to the trading house, taking the printed price (0–4 doubloons) plus 1 (privilege). Then each other player clockwise may sell one good while space remains. Phase ends when everyone has had a turn or the house is full.

Selling rules:
- The trading house holds **four** goods; when full, no more sales this phase.
- It buys only **different** kinds — no duplicates (*exception: office*).
- As a last duty, the trader empties the house **only if it holds four goods**; otherwise the goods remain (making the next trade phase tighter).

Notes: remember small/large market and office. No sale → no extra doubloon. Corn may be sold even for 0.

### The Captain — *ship goods*

**Action:** players **must** load goods on the cargo ships (this action is mandatory).
**Privilege:** the captain takes +1 VP (once, on their first kind loaded).

The captain loads first, then clockwise; the phase continues around the table as long as anyone can still load. A player may get several turns; on each turn they must load if able, but only **one kind of good to one ship** per turn.

Loading rules:
- Each cargo ship carries only **one kind** of good.
- You may not load a kind that is already on another ship.
- You may not load a full ship.
- You must load if you can; load **as many of that kind as fit**, choosing the ship that takes the most (leaving none behind if possible).
- With several loadable kinds, choose freely — you need not maximize barrels.

**Victory points:** 1 VP per **good** loaded (all kinds equal — trader prices don't apply here). The captain's privilege is +1 VP on the first kind only. VP chips are kept **secret**, face-down on the windrose (convert 5×1 → 1×5 when convenient).

**Goods storage (end of captain phase):** each player keeps **one** good on their windrose; all other leftover goods must go into warehouses (small/large) or be returned to the supply. As a last duty, the captain unloads all **full** ships back to the supply; partial/empty ships stay.

Notes: remember small/large warehouse, harbor, wharf. The +1 VP privilege is once only. Goods gained in other phases aren't capacity-limited until the next captain phase.

> **Worked example (4 players).** Anna (captain) has 2 corn + 6 sugar; the 5- and 7-ships are empty and the 6-ship has 3 corn. She loads 6 sugar onto the 7-ship (not the 5-ship — wouldn't fit all 6), earning 6+1 VP. Bob has 2 sugar + 3 tobacco; he tops off the 7-ship with 1 sugar (1 VP), holding tobacco to sell later. Chris loads 1 tobacco on the 5-ship (1 VP). David must load his 1 corn on the 6-ship (no room for his indigo) → 1 VP. Anna again: her 2 corn onto the 6-ship → 2 VP. Bob then must load his 3 tobacco on the 5-ship → 3 VP. Chris and David still have goods but nowhere to load. Loading ends; they store/lose extras. Anna unloads the two full ships (6 and 7); the 5-ship keeps its 4 tobacco.

### The Prospector

**Action:** none.
**Privilege:** 1 doubloon from the bank.

(With 3 players, both prospectors are removed; with 4, one is removed.)

### A New Round

After the last role resolves, the Governor places **one doubloon on each of the three unused placards** (regardless of doubloons already there — accumulated doubloons make placards more attractive). Used placards return to the common area; the Governor passes left; the new Governor opens the next round.

## Game End

The game ends **at the end of the round** in which at least one condition is met:

- At the end of the **mayor phase**, there aren't enough colonists to fill the colonist ship.
- During the **builder phase**, a player builds on their **12th** city space.
- During the **captain phase**, the **last VP chip** is taken (track further VP on paper).

Each player sums: their VP chips + the printed VP of their buildings (red-brown number) + extra VP from their **occupied** large buildings.

> A building scores its printed VP even when unoccupied (so the five large buildings each score 4 base VP regardless). The large buildings' **extra** scoring applies only when occupied.

Most VP wins. Tie-break: most doubloons + goods combined (1 good = 1 doubloon).

## The Buildings

For all buildings:
- Each player may build each building only **once**.
- A building with ≥1 colonist is **occupied**; only occupied buildings function (*exception: VP value*).
- City placement is positional; a building may be moved within the city to make room for a large building, but never removed entirely.
- The red-brown number (upper right) is its game-end VP.
- The number in the first circle is its build cost (irrelevant once built).

### Production buildings (blue, white, light & dark brown)

Together with plantations, these produce goods: indigo plants → indigo (blue); sugar mills → sugar (white); tobacco storage → tobacco (light brown); coffee roasters → coffee (dark brown). **Corn needs no production building** — occupied corn plantations produce corn directly.

The number of circles is the max goods a building can produce when those circles are manned (and you have enough occupied matching plantations for the raw material).

| Production building | Cost | VP | Max output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small indigo plant | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| Indigo plant (large) | 3 | 2 | 3 |
| Small sugar mill | 2 | 1 | 1 |
| Sugar mill (large) | 4 | 2 | 3 |
| Tobacco storage | 5 | 3 | 3 |
| Coffee roaster | 6 | 3 | 2 |

### Small beige buildings

The special functions of beige buildings bend the normal rules; using them is optional (matters for the wharf).

| Building | Cost | VP | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small market | 1 | 1 | +1 doubloon when you sell in the trader phase. |
| Hacienda | 2 | 1 | In the settler phase, before taking a face-up tile, take an extra top-most face-down plantation tile and place it immediately. |
| Construction hut | 2 | 1 | In the settler phase, take a quarry instead of a face-up plantation tile. |
| Small warehouse | 3 | 1 | At end of captain phase, store **all** goods of **one** chosen kind (in addition to the single windrose good). |
| Hospice | 4 | 2 | In the settler phase, when you place a plantation/quarry tile, place a colonist (from supply) on it. |
| Office | 5 | 2 | You may sell a good to the trading house even if that kind is already there (can't sell into a full house). |
| Large market | 5 | 2 | +2 doubloons when you sell in the trader phase (stacks with small market → +3). |
| Large warehouse | 6 | 2 | At end of captain phase, store all goods of **two** chosen kinds (stacks with small warehouse → 3 kinds). |
| Factory | 7 | 3 | In the craftsman phase, earn doubloons by number of **kinds** produced: 2→1, 3→2, 4→3, 5→5. (Quantity irrelevant.) |
| University | 8 | 3 | In the builder phase, place a colonist (from supply) on a building you just built (one colonist even for multi-circle buildings). |
| Harbor | 8 | 3 | +1 VP each time you load goods on a cargo ship in the captain phase. |
| Wharf | 9 | 3 | Once per captain phase, instead of loading a ship, ship **all** of one kind to the supply (imaginary ship, capacity 11) and score VP as normal. |

Hacienda notes: a chosen face-down tile must be placed immediately, not discarded. If you also have an occupied construction hut, you can't substitute a quarry for the face-down tile; a settler with a hacienda takes only one quarry.

### Large buildings

Five large buildings, one each, each occupying two adjacent city spaces but counting as one building. They score base VP unoccupied; the **extra** scoring below requires occupation.

| Building | Cost | Base VP | Extra at game end (if occupied) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Guild hall | 10 | 4 | +1 VP per small production building, +2 VP per large production building (occupied or not). |
| Residence | 10 | 4 | +4 / 5 / 6 / 7 VP for ≤9 / 10 / 11 / 12 filled island spaces. |
| Fortress | 10 | 4 | +1 VP per 3 colonists on your board. |
| Customs house | 10 | 4 | +1 VP per 4 VP **chips** earned (buildings' VP excluded). |
| City hall | 10 | 4 | +1 VP per beige building in your city (city hall itself counts). |

## The Game for Two

All basic rules apply, with these changes:

- Each player takes 1 player board, 3 doubloons, 1 plantation (start player: indigo + Governor placard; 2nd player: corn).
- **Remove 3 of each** plantation/quarry tile from the game. Shuffle the rest; lay out 3 face-up (players + 1).
- Buildings on the board: **1 of each beige**, **2 of each** of the six production buildings.
- VP: **65 total**.
- Colonists: **40** supply + **2** on the ship (the two-player minimum).
- Cargo ships: the **4-** and **6-**space ships.
- Goods: **remove 2 of each** kind; the rest form the supply.
- Use the trading house and **7 role placards** (remove one prospector).

**Play:** the Governor chooses a role (executed by both players). Players alternate choosing until each has taken **three** roles (six of the seven resolved). The Governor puts 1 doubloon on the remaining (seventh) placard, players return theirs, the Governor passes, and play continues.

**Game end:** same triggers as the basic game.

## Expansion I: The New Buildings

All original rules apply, with these changes.

**Preparation.** Place all production buildings as usual. Lay out the **55 beige buildings** (2 each of 12 small + 5 large originals, plus 2 each of 12 small + 2 large new violet-ringed buildings). Starting with the start player and going clockwise, players each choose **1 building** and place it on a matching-cost empty beige space; small buildings are placed in **pairs** (the second of the kind on top — except in a 2-player game). Continue until all 12 small and 5 large beige spaces are filled. For the 2-, 5-, and 8-doubloon costs, players choose **two different** of the four available at each cost. Return the 26 unchosen beige buildings to the box.

> Because each cost-space holds only one kind, early choices can lock others out (e.g. if both 2-cost spaces are taken by black market and hacienda, the construction hut and forest house can't enter the game).

| Building | Cost | VP | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aqueduct | 1 | 1 | Producing ≥1 indigo in the **large** indigo plant grants +1 indigo; likewise +1 sugar for the **large** sugar mill. (No effect on small versions.) |
| Forest house | 2 | 1 | In the settler phase, place a **forest** tile on an empty island space instead of a plantation/quarry. Each **two** forests reduce a build cost by 1 doubloon (no column cap; stacks with builder/quarry reductions). Forests hold no colonists and can't be converted to/from plantations. |
| Black market | 2 | 1 | When building, reduce cost by up to 3 by returning up to one each of a colonist, a good, and/or a VP. You may keep no doubloons after building (proceeds must go entirely toward the build). |
| Storehouse | 3 | 1 | At end of each captain phase, keep **three** extra goods of any kinds (beyond the normal one). |
| Guest house | 4 | 2 | In the mayor phase, place up to **two** colonists here as "guests"; move them freely to any building/plantation/quarry before, during, or after other phases (they work immediately, staying until the next mayor phase). |
| Trading post | 5 | 2 | In the trader phase, sell to your own trading post instead of the trading house: any good (even a duplicate) at normal price + trader bonus, placed directly in the supply. (Markets don't add their bonus here.) *Recommended: use either office or trading post, not both.* |
| Church | 5 | 2 | When you build from columns 2/3 take 1 VP chip; from column 4 take 2 VP chips. (No VP for building the church itself.) |
| Small wharf | 6 | 2 | Once in the captain phase, ship **different** goods on one turn, scoring **1 VP per two** goods shipped to the supply; ship as few (≥1) or as many as you like. (Usable alongside the wharf.) |
| Lighthouse | 7 | 3 | +1 doubloon each time you load a cargo ship in the captain phase; +1 more if you're the captain (and the captain takes 1 doubloon whether they ship or not). |
| Specialty factory | 8 | 3 | In the craftsman phase, take doubloons equal to **(largest single-kind production − 1)**, excluding corn. |
| Library | 8 | 3 | **Double** your role privilege. (Settler: take a plantation or quarry first, then a second plantation after others choose — not a second quarry. Craftsman: take two different extra goods.) |
| Union hall | 9 | 3 | Before your first shipping in the captain phase, take 1 VP chip per **2 of the same good** on your windrose, then ship normally. |
| Statue | 10 | 8 | Holds no colonists; worth 8 VP at game end. |
| Cloister | 10 | 4 | At game end, score for sets of 3 like island tiles: +1 / 3 / 6 / 10 VP for 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 sets (max 10). |

## Expansion II: The Nobles

The 7 new beige buildings + 1 new production building combine with the 17 base and 14 Expansion I beige buildings; place them on their cost spaces. Place **20 nobles** by the colonist supply.

**Nobles.** With this expansion, exhausting the colonist supply (or noble supply) **no longer triggers game end** — all colonists are usable. At game start and at the end of each mayor phase, place **one noble** on the colonist ship in place of one colonist (while nobles remain). Nobles are taken from the ship like colonists, can be placed anywhere/anytime a colonist can, occupy buildings identically, and are each worth **1 VP** at game end. A noble and a colonist are interchangeable **except** where a building specifies a different result for each — in which case a colonist can't get the noble result and vice-versa.

> General note: where original/Expansion I text says "colonist," read "colonist/noble" when playing with this expansion.

| Building | Cost | VP | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Land office | 2 | 1 | In the trader phase: **colonist**-occupied → buy the top face-down plantation for 1 doubloon and place it; **noble**-occupied → discard a plantation from your island for 1 doubloon. |
| Chapel | 3 | 1 | Each craftsman phase: **colonist**-occupied → +1 doubloon; **noble**-occupied → +1 VP. |
| Hunting lodge | 4 | 2 | End of each settler phase: **colonist**-occupied → discard a plantation from your island; **noble**-occupied → +2 VP if you (uniquely) have the most empty island spaces (ties get nothing). |
| Zoning office | 5 | 2 | **Colonist**-occupied → −1 doubloon building small buildings (cols 1–3); **noble**-occupied → −2 doubloons building large buildings (col 4). |
| Royal supplier | 6 | 2 | On your first shipping turn (before shipping), supply the royal warehouse with up to "number of nobles on your island" goods (all different kinds) for +1 VP each. No other bonuses apply to these goods. |
| Villa | 7 | 3 | On your first mayor-phase turn, take 1 noble from the **supply** (not the ship) instead of a colonist; if none remain, take a colonist instead. |
| Jeweler | 8 | 3 | In the craftsman phase, +1 doubloon per noble on your island. (Counts as a large production building for the guild hall.) |
| Royal garden | 10 | 4 | At game end, +1 VP per noble on your island (so each such noble is worth 2 VP total). |

## Edition Differences (cross-check)

The two uploaded editions describe the same game. Differences found:

| Topic | Deluxe edition | Original 2002 edition |
|---|---|---|
| Special-building color | **beige** | **violet** |
| Spare-colonist holding area | **windrose** | **San Juan** |
| VP chip mix | 31 × 1 + 19 × 5 = **126** total; 5-player uses all 126 | 32 × 1 + 18 × 5 = **122** total; 5-player uses all 122 |
| Goods tokens (coffee/tobacco) | described as "9 of coffee and tobacco" | same (9 each); both list 10 corn / 11 sugar / 11 indigo |
| Building-VP number location | upper **right** corner | buildings section says upper right, but the game-end section says upper **left** (internal typo in the original; the reference card shows it upper right) |
| Production note | implicit | adds an explicit note that raw materials don't exist as tokens and unmanned circles produce nothing |
| Expansions | includes 2-player rules + Expansion I + Expansion II | base game only |

All role actions, privileges, phase sequencing, building effects, the captain-phase example, the 2-player setup, and the three game-end triggers match between editions. (The only numeric discrepancy worth flagging for design reference is the **122 vs 126** total VP between editions.)
